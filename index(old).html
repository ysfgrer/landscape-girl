About the author

Landscape Girl is an observer, not a narrator. She collects what lingers after the talk is over, decoding the unwritten rules of the studio and the reality checks of the profession.

the queen studio note

There are those who navigate the academic space, and then there are those who actively shape how you perceive it. Spotted:

Ebru Erbaş Gürler

DIVA Landscape Designer

what stayed with me after her talk...

The department knew exactly what it was doing when they brought her in. My very first studio was with Proffessor Gurler, and her style, presence, and command of the room were an education in themselves. She sets a tone that demands you take your own work seriously. But beyond the impeccable studio presence, it’s the professional lore she shared that really stays with you—a masterclass in the unglamorous realities of practice.

Looks like her early career featured a classic landscape tragedy. She designed a garden for a family acquaintance, delivered the meticulous drawings, and then... lost contact. No payment ever came. Only later did she realize the client had simply taken her drawings and built the garden without her. And if that wasn’t enough of a lesson in the invisibility of our labor, there was another sighting: an architect who allegedly presented herself as a landscape architect, taking projects drawn by Ebru’s team and publishing them under her own name. Authorship in this field? Not to be dramatic, but it’s a battlefield.

Hearing these stories forces a reflection on the alternative paths our careers can take. It makes you realize how easily a trajectory can shift based on who you trust, and how we must learn just as fiercely from the stolen gardens as we do from the celebrated ones. A massive wave of gratitude to our speakers for dropping the facade and serving the reality check we all needed.

xoxo, landscape girl

Damla Turan

career sighting

what stayed with me after her talk...

Navigating the post-grad world is less about waiting for the perfect job to appear and more about aggressively carving out your own footing. Damla's professional journey is a testament to the fact that career paths in landscape architecture are rarely linear; they require a constant, strategic recalibration of your skills and your environment.

What stayed with me wasn't just the highlights reel of her resume, but the quiet, undeniable resilience required to make a name for yourself in rooms that are already full. She offered a look into the stamina it takes to endure the less glamorous phases of practice before you hit your stride. Lowkey iconic to just lay out the blueprint for survival like that.

studio note

what stayed with me after her talk...

There is a specific art to translating a complex, multi-layered landscape into a digestible professional narrative, and Gülce’s trajectory proves she has mastered that translation.
